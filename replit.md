# Workspace

## Overview

pnpm workspace monorepo using TypeScript. Each package manages its own dependencies.

## Stack

- **Monorepo tool**: pnpm workspaces
- **Node.js version**: 24
- **Package manager**: pnpm
- **TypeScript version**: 5.9
- **API framework**: Express 5
- **Database**: PostgreSQL + Drizzle ORM
- **Validation**: Zod (`zod/v4`), `drizzle-zod`
- **API codegen**: Orval (from OpenAPI spec)
- **Build**: esbuild (CJS bundle)

## Key Commands

- `pnpm run typecheck` — full typecheck across all packages
- `pnpm run build` — typecheck + build all packages
- `pnpm --filter @workspace/api-spec run codegen` — regenerate API hooks and Zod schemas from OpenAPI spec
- `pnpm --filter @workspace/db run push` — push DB schema changes (dev only)
- `pnpm --filter @workspace/api-server run dev` — run API server locally

See the `pnpm-workspace` skill for workspace structure, TypeScript setup, and package details.

## Artifacts

### `artifacts/gta-clone` — South Island City
A top-down 2D crime/driving game in the spirit of GTA1. Pure Canvas 2D — every sprite is bezier-path drawn (no images, no 3D). React + Vite + TypeScript.

Game systems (all in `src/game/`):
- `world.ts` — procedural city, deterministic from seed:
  - **Districts**: 4×4 macro-grid carves the map into named zones — downtown (tall dark glass towers, lots of neon), commercial (mid-rise, warm walls, neon signs), residential (low warm-toned houses, almost no neon), industrial (gray/brown low blocks), park (mostly grass with the occasional building), waterfront (sandy beach houses on the bay).
  - **Building variety**: each block is built from a district-specific palette and height range, and large blocks have a chance to be subdivided into two smaller buildings with a 1-tile alley between them.
  - **River + bridges**: a 5-tile-wide river slices horizontally across the city. Where the major vertical avenues cross it, the road tiles become bridges with stone railings on both sides; otherwise the band is animated water with rippling waves and sparkles.
  - **Beaches**: tiles directly adjacent to the river on land turn into pebbly sand strips.
  - **Plazas + fountains**: each park block carries a stone-tiled plaza tile in the middle, where an animated 3-tier fountain (stone ring, ripples, central jet, splash droplets) is placed.
- `sprites.ts` — bezier car/human drawing with police lightbar flash, fire overlays, walk cycles.
- `render.ts` — tile renderer with extruded buildings (NW lighting), shadows, particles, rain, day/night, streetlight halos, lightning.
- `physics.ts` — realistic vehicle dynamics: speed-graded steering (tighter turns at low speed, looser at highway speed), throttle envelope (peak torque rolls off near top speed), engine braking when off-throttle, quadratic air drag, speed-graded grip with drift slip when over-cornering, separate **handbrake** (Shift — locks rear, kills lateral grip for slides) vs foot brake, smart throttle/brake auto-reverse, and crash damage scaled to kinetic-energy delta.
  - **Vehicle vs world**: sub-stepped (6 px chunks) so a fast car can't tunnel through a TILE-thick wall; per-axis sliding within each chunk so cars scrape along walls instead of catching on them.
  - **Vehicle vs vehicle**: full SAT (Separating Axis Theorem) on rotated rectangles — projects each car's 4 corners onto both bodies' axes and finds the minimum-translation vector. Replaces the old circle-circle hack that used `length/2` as radius (which made side-by-side cars constantly "collide" and missed real corner-to-corner crunches). Push-apart is mass-weighted so trucks barely flinch from hatchbacks; impulse uses (1 + restitution) along the contact normal; damage scales with squared closing speed; impact sparks spawn at the actual contact point.
  - **Vehicle vs human**: point-in-rotated-rectangle test — transforms the pedestrian into the car's local frame and checks against the real OBB. The old circle hitbox of radius `length/2 + 3` would "hit" peds standing beside a parked car. Slow-speed contacts now push the ped out the shortest overlap axis instead of through the car.
  - Bullets are sub-stepped (6 px raycast) against `isSolidAt` and stop at building walls with spark + smoke impact.
- `ai.ts` — strategic AI:
  - **Civilians**: lane-follow with right-side offset, brake at intersections, yield to crossing traffic, signal turns; pull over (right + brake + signal) when a chasing patrol car closes from behind. **Bail out**: if their car catches fire or drops below 18% hp, they jump out the left side, panic, and run.
  - **Pedestrians**: walk sidewalks, dodge oncoming cars, panic from gunshots, panic-contagion to nearby peds, witness mode (run to nearest cop and report → wanted increases). **Gawkers**: peds within ~90 px of a fresh corpse stop, turn to face it for ~1.5 s, then panic-flee from it (still spreads via contagion).
  - **Gangs**: claim turf (only aggro near their home anchor unless wanted ≥ 4), strafe-shoot with lead prediction, take cover behind parked cars, low-HP retreat (back away & partial heal). **Call for backup**: when a gang triggers attack, up to 4 nearby gang members in the same turf wake up and join. **Bounding overwatch**: when multiple gangs are attacking together, they alternate 1.6 s phases — half SUPPRESS (heavy fire from cover/strafe) while the other half ADVANCE (push toward the player at a flanking angle, fire less); roles flip so the squad rolls forward in pairs.
  - **Police on foot**: squad roles assigned by index — suppressor (holds back, fires often), flanker (90° crossfire), cutoff (blocks predicted player path), reserve (closes from opposite side); lead-aim shooting; commandeer parked patrol cars at high wanted; bust player on foot if close & slow. **Cover**: when player is shooting back at wanted ≥ 2, suppressors and reserves seek cover behind nearby vehicles, peek-fire accurately, and break cover when the player flees or stops shooting.
  - **Police pursuit driving**: per-car strategies — tail / cutoff to intersection ahead / parallel chase LEFT side / **box-in** parallel RIGHT side (cars 2 + 3 pinch the player between two lanes) / trail support; PIT maneuver into rear corner of player car at high speed; drive-by shoot at wanted ≥ 3.
  - **Vehicle stuck recovery**: any AI-driven car that has been requesting throttle but moving slower than 18 px/s for >1.2 s slams into reverse for 0.8 s with random steer, popping itself off curbs, props, or jammed corners.
  - **Roadblocks**: at wanted ≥ 3, parked patrol car + 2 cops occasionally spawn at an intersection ~600 px ahead of player travel direction.
- `audio.ts` — Web Audio synthesized music with crossfaded mood layers (calm/chase/combat/radio) and SFX (gunshot, explosion, hit, pickup). Master bus runs through a soft-knee compressor so SFX peaks don't clip; all music layers share an algorithmic-IR convolution reverb send. Each mood now has a real arrangement: **calm** = three detuned saws through a slow LFO-swept lowpass, cycling Am/F/G/Am chord progression; **chase** = square bass arp + 8th-note hi-hat + snare on beats 2/4; **combat** = sub kick on every quarter, snare backbeat, and a distorted saw lead playing an A-minor riff; **radio** = walking triangle bass + square chord stabs on the off-beats + shaker. All patterns share an A-minor tonal centre so crossfades stay consonant.
- `game.ts` — main tick loop: spawn/despawn, weather, time-of-day, wanted system (0–6 stars), score, money, music mood selection. Manages animals, bird flocks, props, wind. Plays a 1.4s player death animation (body slumps, blood pool grows, camera zooms in, red vignette) before the WASTED overlay.
- **Wildlife**: stray dogs and cats wander sidewalks/parks, flee from gunshots & speeding cars; pigeon clusters peck the ground and take flight (with feather puff) when scared. Distant bird flocks fly overhead in V-formation casting moving ground shadows.
- **Environment props**: 4 tree species — generic deciduous (multi-lobe canopy with highlight), oak (broad canopy with knotted trunk), pine (stacked triangle conifer), palm (curved trunk + radial fronds + coconuts); plus bushes (some with red berries), colorful flower patches (red/yellow/purple/pink), tall grass blade tufts, and rare desert cactus. Sidewalks get fire hydrants, mailboxes, trash cans, and lamp posts. Trees and bushes accumulate snow caps during snow; lamps glow at dusk/night.
- **Detailed character figures**: humans have shoes, tapered shaded legs, belt, lit shoulder + shadowed chest, biceps + forearm + visible hand, neck, jawline shading, cheek highlight, ear, sculpted hair with side parting. Police wear a cap with gold badge, bulletproof vest, chest badge and radio. Gang members wear a red bandana and tank top. Weapons (pistol, SMG, shotgun) are drawn with grip, slide, magazine, and stock.
- **Weather**: clear / rain / storm / fog / **snow** (drifting flakes + cool tint + snow caps on trees) / **wind** (swirling colored leaves blow across the screen). Wind direction & strength is global and drifts over time; it influences how snow, leaves, and feathers move.
- `input.ts` — keyboard (WASD/arrows, E enter/exit/use, Space fire, Shift handbrake) + mouse aim/click.
- **Enterable shops**: ~13 procedurally-placed storefronts (`Shop[]` on world) — Hospital (heal $50), Gun Shop (pistol ammo $100), Ammu-Nation (SMG $200), Burger Shot (free food, +25 HP), Pay 'n' Spray (respray car + clear wanted, $100, must be in vehicle), Safehouse (set respawn, free). Each is scored to a real building by district, color, footprint and given a door tile + facing. `render.ts/drawShopFront` paints a colored door, glowing sign with the shop name, animated sidewalk ring, and a brighter "active" highlight when the player is on the door tile.
- **Walk-in interiors** (`game/interior.ts`): when on foot, pressing E at a shop teleports the player into a fully-rendered top-down interior room (360x260) with type-specific decor — hospital (beds, IV stands, supply cabinets, doctor NPC), gun shop / ammu (counters, weapon racks, target posters, clerk), burger shot (booths, kitchen counter, fryer, drink machine, cashier), safehouse (bed, sofa, TV, fridge). Player walks WASD with wall + prop collision, walks onto the south doormat or back through the door to exit (returns to the original world door tile). E at the counter triggers the shop's action (heal/buy/respawn-set/eat) with a particle flash and ding. NPCs wander idly. The world ticks pause while inside; the HUD swaps the world prompt for an interior banner with the shop name + controls hint. Pay 'n' Spray still uses the brief overlay since the player must be in a vehicle. Money drops from kills ($75 cop / $50 gang / $15 ped); player respawns at the last safehouse if one was visited.

- **Mission system** (`game.ts/tickMissions`): up to 2 jobs spawn into the world over time; 5 mission types — **reach** (drive/run to a marker), **collect** (grab a money pickup), **destroy** (wreck a target vehicle), **eliminate** (kill a target gang/cop), **escape** (survive N seconds with raised wanted level). Each mission carries name/description/reward/score/markerColor. `render.ts/drawMissionMarkers` paints world-space pulsing color pillars over targets; `drawObjectiveArrow` draws an off-screen edge arrow with distance to the active mission. `HUD.tsx` shows an active-mission card (top-right under the wanted stars) with name, description, reward, distance, and an escape timer when relevant; the minimap also draws filled mission dots and an outlined current target with a dashed sight line from the player.
- **Lighting overhaul** (`render.ts`): replaced the flat dark blue night overlay with a radial vignette (lighter at the player center, much darker at the edges); a subtle screen vignette is on at all times for cinematic mood. `drawNightLights` adds streetlamp halos with a hot core, deterministically lit building windows (per-building seed so they don't flicker random), real headlight CONES projected forward from each car as wedge gradients (additive blend), tail-light glow, and brighter police strobe halos. Dusk also fires a half-strength pass so headlights show up before full night. `sprites.ts/drawCar` now takes an `isNight` flag and draws bigger additive headlight + tail-light caps so cars read clearly at night.
- **Figure polish**: human sprite gained sole highlights, knee creases, elbow joint dots, finger hints; the head now has an eye (white + pupil), brow line, nose hint, and a small mouth. Cops added shoulder rank stripes and a belt holster; gang members added a gold chain and a bicep tattoo; pedestrians have 5 deterministic outfit variants (open jacket, brimmed cap, backpack, glasses, plain) so crowds look mixed.
- **Car & building textures**: car body now uses a vertical paint gradient (sky highlight on top → shadow on bottom) for a proper metallic feel, plus a darker bumper bar, vertical-slat front grille, dark rocker panel skirt under the doors, an exhaust tip on the rear, a windshield reflection sheen, a roof spec streak, and richer wheels with chrome hubcaps + cross spokes + sidewall ring. Buildings got a vertical wall gradient (top brighter, bottom darker), a foundation/kick strip and ambient-occlusion shadow at ground level. Brick walls run a proper running-bond pattern with per-course tint variation, occasional darker stained bricks, and lighter corner quoins. Concrete panel walls add a horizontal floor seam every 24px, rust-drip weathering streaks, and detailed base vents. Glass towers add vertical mullion columns and a strong moving reflection band by day.
- **Fistfighting & reaction rules** (`game.ts/tryPunch`, `ai.ts/updateFighter`, `audio.ts/playPunch`, `sprites.ts` punch animation): Space/click with `weapon === "fist"` swings a melee punch (cone in front, ~14px range humans / ~18px cars, 0.42s cooldown, 22 dmg). Sprite extends the lead arm forward into a clenched fist with knuckle line + "speed line" arc at peak. New `aiState: "fight"` — angry actors charge the player and throw their own punches (8 dmg, 0.85s cooldown, hp-knockback + camera shake) until they take ~35% damage or 5–6s elapses, then revert to panic. Reaction rules when **player** punches: pedestrian → 35% fight back / 65% panic + small wanted bump; cop → wanted+2 + immediate chase + "Assaulting an officer!" notification; gang → switch to gun attack; civilian car → driver bails-and-fights 25% / flees in car otherwise; gang car → driver bails out shooting; cop car → wanted+1. Cars take small dent damage and a knockback bump per hit. AI vs AI fistfights don't escalate the wanted level. Punch SFX is a low triangle thump + low-passed noise whoosh.
- **Crowd & traffic chaos reduction** (`ai.ts`): tightened panic contagion (radius 100→55px, ~50%/s probabilistic catch, requires the original threat source to still be nearby — stops permanent chain-panic across the map); panic flee speed dialed down (1.7→1.35x, wobble 0.4→0.22) so peds don't dart into traffic. Civilian cars now PULL OVER for combat (slow + drift right + signal) within 220px instead of flooring throttle within 350px (the old rule caused mass pile-ups every gunshot). Per-frame honk probabilities converted to per-second rates (was effectively 3 honks/s, now ~1/s). Intersection yield now has a deterministic tie-break (lower vehicle id has right of way; stationary cars are ignored) so two cars don't deadlock at a corner. Pedestrians now check for fast oncoming cars before stepping off the curb onto a road tile and wait at the edge if one is bearing down on them.

UI: `components/GameCanvas.tsx` runs the loop; `HUD.tsx` shows wealth/HP/ammo/wanted/combo/active-mission/minimap (with shop + mission dots) + shop entry prompt + in-shop overlay; `TitleOverlay.tsx` is the start screen.
